1
No reviews on the page Critical
The page says "No reviews yet." At $1,210/night, the absence of any social proof is a major trust gap. Visitors have no validation that others had a good experience. Even 2–3 reviews would significantly lift conversion.
2
Single high-commitment CTA Critical
"Book Now" requires selecting dates and committing before enquiring. Most visitors to a $1,200/night property are in research mode — they need a "Check Availability" or "Ask a Question" path before they're ready to book.
3
Weak headline — no value proposition
The H1 is simply "Surf House." It says nothing about the experience, the location, or why this property is worth $1,210/night. A visitor who lands here from a search or social ad gets zero context above the fold.
4
Generic description copy
"A luxurious haven located in the heart of Hartswell" is technically accurate but doesn't sell the dream. Luxury rental buyers want to feel the experience before they buy it. The copy reads like a listing template, not an invitation.
5
Price shown without context
$1,210/night looks steep in isolation. Split across 8 guests, it's $151/person — which is competitive. There's no per-person framing, no "includes" list, and no explanation of what makes this price justified.
6
Trust badges buried in global nav only
The 2025 Emmy Award and 100 Collection membership are significant trust signals — but they only appear in the header area. They should be on the property card itself, where buying decisions are made.
7
No urgency or scarcity signals
Nothing on the page tells the visitor to act now. Luxury rental pages that show availability windows ("limited dates in June") or popularity signals ("high demand") consistently convert better than static listings.

Recommendation

Where to Start

All three issues are real — but not all are equally urgent. Here's the suggested order.

⭐ Start Here

Test Variant 2 (Inquiry-First) first

The biggest drop-off on a $1,210/night property happens at the CTA. Most visitors are in research mode — they're not ready to select dates and click "Book Now" on a first visit. Variant 2 gives them a lower-friction path. Every inquiry becomes a sales conversation that Isaac can close through HubSpot. If inquiry rate goes up, the other variants become less urgent — you've already fixed the funnel.

1
Implement Variant 2 changes on the live Surf House page
Split "Book Now" into "Check Availability" + "Ask a Concierge". Add the inline inquiry form. Takes 1–2 hours in Elementor/WordPress.
2
Run the weekly cycle script after 7 days
GA4 will capture inquiry events (form submits). Compare inquiry rate week-over-week in the tracker. This is your signal.
3
If inquiry rate improves → roll Variant 1 changes in next
Add trust badges + urgency signals. Once you have the inquiry pipeline flowing, social proof helps close the ones still on the fence.
4
Test Variant 3 copy on a property with more traffic
Experience-led copy needs traffic volume to show statistical signal. Apply it to your highest-traffic property page first for faster results.

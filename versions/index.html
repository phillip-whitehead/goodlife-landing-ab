Arsh Sanwarwala / ThrillX — CRO Principles Applied

Each version tests exactly one hypothesis. This is the core ThrillX discipline: isolate the variable, measure the result, combine the winners.

Principle 1

Above the fold is everything

Headline + hero image tests produce 50%+ conversion swings. Tested in v2 with sensory copy and credibility cluster.

Principle 2

Lower-commitment CTAs win

"Learn More" beat "Book A Call" and drove 385% more booked calls. Tested in v4 with exploratory language throughout.

Principle 3

Fewer CTAs = better

Cutting from two CTAs to one produces measurable lifts. Applied in v2 and v4 — single hero CTA only.

Principle 4

Address visitor anxieties directly

Pricing transparency, cancellation policy, what's included. Tested in v3 with trust strip, FAQ, and micro-copy.

Principle 5

Review mining

Use real guest language in copy — motivation, anxiety, value themes. Applied in v3/v5: "like a cool cousin who knows everyone."

Principle 6

Shorter forms

Remove every non-essential field. Tested in v4: name + email only, replacing full booking widget.

Principle 7

One hypothesis per version

Don't change everything at once — you won't know what worked. v2/v3/v4/v6 each isolate one variable.

Framework

"Beauty and Brains"

Professional visual design + data-driven optimization. Don't sacrifice aesthetics for CRO — both matter together.
